Wedding Schedule

All events are listed in local India time (IST - GMT+5:30)

The primary wedding events begin with the Seemant Poojan on March 11 and end with the After-Party on March 12. However, depending on your availability and presence in Mumbai, we'd also love to have you attend any/all of the pre- and post-wedding events!

March 09, 2026 - Pre-Wedding Ceremonies

Grahamakh

[Time - 09:00 AM]

Venue: Shubhankar's Home

A ritual where the bride and groom's families invoke harmony with the planets for a successfull obstacle-free wedding ceremony. This pooja is performed separately in both homes.

Attire: No dress code.

March 10, 2026 - Pre-Wedding Ceremonies

Mehendi

[Time - 04:00 PM -07:00 PM]

Venue: Shubhankar's Home

A very informal event where henna designs are traditionally applied to the hands and feet. Women also choose colorful bangles to wear for the wedding day.

Attire: No dress code but wear something comfortable that you don't mind getting henna on!

March 11, 2026 - Pre-Wedding Ceremonies

Halad

[Time - 08:30 AM - 10:30 AM]

Venue: Shubhankar's Home

During this ceremony, a turmeric paste is applied to the bride and groom, often in their respective homes. Guests are welcome to participate!

Attire: No dress code but it is common to wear yellow.

Seemant Poojan

[Time - 05:00 PM]

Venue: Kakaji Ni Wadi, Panvel

Traditional ceremony where the groom's family is symbolically welcomed at the wedding venue by the bride's family.

Attire: Women: salwar kameez; please reach out to Shubhankar/Juie and we can help you make arrangements! Men: smart casual (light shirt and trousers; no jacket needed)

Sangeet

[Time - 07:15 PM]

Venue: Kakaji Ni Wadi, Panvel

The Sangeet is a musical celebration. We'll start with some brief dancing, featuring one confident performer (Juie) and one amateur (Shubhankar), before professional artists take over with a concert.

Attire: Same as the Seemant Poojan.

March 12, 2026 - Wedding Ceremony

Lagna (Wedding Ceremony)

[Time - 08:00 AM]

Venue: Kakaji Ni Wadi, Panvel

The main wedding ceremony with traditional Maharashtrian rituals under a mandap (wedding canopy).

Attire: Formal traditional Indian wear. Women: sarees, lehengas. Men: sherwanis, kurtas. Again, please reach out to Shubhankar/Juie and we can help you make arrangements!
